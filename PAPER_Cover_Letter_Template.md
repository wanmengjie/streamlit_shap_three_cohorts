We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled **"Development and validation of a prediction model for incident depression–cognition comorbidity in older adults in China: a CHARLS-based study with exploration of potential intervention effects"** for consideration for publication in [Journal Name].

Depression and cognitive impairment frequently co-occur in older adults, yet few studies have integrated prediction modeling with causal inference to identify modifiable intervention targets. Our study addresses this gap by developing a three-cohort framework using the China Health and Retirement Longitudinal Study (CHARLS) to:

1. **Predict** incident depression–cognition comorbidity in three distinct baseline populations (healthy, depression-only, cognition-impaired-only);
2. **Estimate causal effects** of five modifiable factors (exercise, drinking, social isolation, normal BMI, low chronic disease burden) using XLearner, with rigorous assumption checks (overlap, covariate balance, E-value);
3. **Cross-validate** causal estimates with propensity score matching and weighting.

Our main finding is that **regular exercise significantly reduces incident comorbidity risk in older adults with depression only** (ATE = −4.2%, 95% CI: −7.9% to −0.9%, p=0.019), with overlap satisfied, moderate covariate balance, and E-value of 2.0. This result is supported by cross-validation with PSM and PSW, which showed directionally consistent protective effects. The integrated approach can inform risk stratification and targeted interventions for older adults in China and similar settings.
